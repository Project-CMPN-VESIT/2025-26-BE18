Groundwater Level Analysis for Kalyan

Understanding past trends and future outlook

Executive Summary

Kalyan's groundwater situation has consistently been rated as "Safe" across all available data years. This means the area effectively replenishes its groundwater reserves, exceeding the amount extracted for various uses. While there was a significantly higher rainfall and groundwater extraction (draft) in 2016-17, the trends from 2019-20 onwards show a relatively stable pattern of recharge and draft, with a slight, gradual decrease in recharge. Overall, the region maintains a healthy and sustainable groundwater balance.

Key Insights

  • Consistently "Safe": Kalyan has consistently been in the "Safe" category, indicating that groundwater extraction is well within sustainable limits, and there's no immediate risk of over-extraction.
  • Healthy Balance (Recharge vs. Draft): Groundwater Recharge (water naturally refilling the ground) has always been much higher than the Draft (water extracted), ensuring a continuous and healthy supply.
  • Significant Shift in 2016-17: The year 2016-17 stands out with exceptionally high rainfall (over 6300 mm) and a much higher draft (around 394 Million Cubic Meters - MCM) compared to subsequent years. This suggests a unique hydrological or usage pattern during that period.
  • Recent Stability (2019-20 onwards): From 2019-20 to 2024-25, rainfall figures are shown as constant (around 2491 mm). During this period, groundwater recharge shows a slight but gradual downward trend (from ~866 MCM to ~851 MCM), while draft remains relatively stable (around 163-165 MCM).
  • Sustainability: Despite the slight decrease in recharge in recent years, the overall balance remains strongly positive, confirming the area's sustainable groundwater use.
