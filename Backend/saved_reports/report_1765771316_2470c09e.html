Impact of Land Use Change on Groundwater Sustainability

Analysis of Lucknow Area (2008-2016) using Earth Observation Data

Pradipika Verma, Prafull Singh, S. K. Srivastava

Study Overview

This study investigates the long-term effects of Land Use/Land Cover (LULC) changes on surface and groundwater resources in the Quaternary aquifers of the Lucknow area, Ganga plain, from 2008 to 2016. Using multi-temporal satellite data (Landsat-7 & 8) and field surveys, the research maps LULC and hydro-geomorphic features, analyzes groundwater level trends, and explores their interrelationships.

  • Location: Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India
  • Period: 2008 - 2016
  • Key Data: Landsat Satellite Data, Groundwater Levels, Meteorological Data
  • Methods: Hybrid Image Classification, IDW Interpolation, GIS Analysis

Overall Key Findings (2008-2016)

LULC Changes:

  • Built-up land: Increased by 4.09% (176.38 to 279.89 km²)
  • Open land/construction: Increased by 5.49% (195.45 to 334.37 km²)
  • Vegetation cover: Decreased by 8.80% (434.33 to 211.71 km²)
  • Agricultural land: Decreased by 2.31% (1407.8 to 1349.49 km²)
  • Surface water bodies: Decreased by 0.35% (24.53 to 15.77 km²)
  • Forest/orchard: Increased by 1.87% (290.09 to 337.42 km²)

Hydro-Geomorphic & Groundwater:

  • Total hydro-geomorphic features: Decreased from 53.02 km² to 34.24 km²
  • Paleochannels: Decreased from 24.13 km² to 15.34 km²
  • Oxbow lakes: Decreased from 6.78 km² to 5.23 km²
  • Water bodies (specific): Decreased from 17.46 km² to 8.98 km²
  • Groundwater Depletion: Significant decrease in groundwater levels observed.
  • Area with DTWL 5-10 mbgl: Increased from 504 km² to 645 km²
  • Area with DTWL >35 mbgl: Increased from 1 km² to 2 km² (indicating deeper water tables)
  • A significant positive correlation (R²=0.81) found between LULC changes and groundwater level dynamics.

Key Action Items & Recommendations

  • Urgent Resource Inventory: Need for further inventory, rejuvenation, and restoration of all important land and water bodies to ensure future water and food security.
  • Sustainable Urban Planning: Critical to determine land use activities that pose a danger to surface water bodies and hydro-morphological features.
  • Groundwater Management: Rejuvenation of major tributaries of the Gomati River basin is essential for improved groundwater resource management.
  • Data-Driven Decision Making: The study confirms the value of geospatial technology for mapping and generating a database to support water resource management and urban planning.
  • Addressing Anthropogenic Pressure: Recognize and mitigate the impact of population growth, urbanization, and agricultural expansion on water resources.
  • Impervious Surface Reduction: Strategies to increase natural infiltration and surface recharge by reducing impervious surfaces created by built-up land expansion.
