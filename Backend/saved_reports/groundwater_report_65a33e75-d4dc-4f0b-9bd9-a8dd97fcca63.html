Groundwater Status: Shirol

Executive Summary

The groundwater situation in **Shirol** is currently **healthy and stable**. The area is categorized as 'safe', meaning the amount of water extracted from underground is well within sustainable limits compared to how much it gets refilled. This ensures a reliable water supply for the community and its activities without overstressing the natural resources.

Key Insights

  • Safe Category: Shirol falls into the 'safe' category, indicating a good balance between groundwater recharge and extraction.
  • Low Extraction Rate: Only about 38.57% of the available groundwater is currently being extracted. This is significantly below the threshold for concern, highlighting sustainable usage.
  • Ample Future Availability: There's a substantial amount of groundwater (approximately 6646.66 units) available for future use, suggesting good long-term water security.
  • Diverse Recharge Sources: Groundwater levels are replenished through various sources, with significant contributions from agricultural practices (return flow from irrigation) and natural rainfall. Surface irrigation also plays a major role in recharging the underground water.
  • Agricultural Dependence: Agriculture is both a major contributor to groundwater recharge (through irrigation return flow) and the primary user of groundwater in the region.
  • No Historical Data: Unfortunately, specific year-on-year groundwater level data was not provided. Therefore, we cannot show past trends or predict future groundwater levels. This analysis is based on the most recent available data.
