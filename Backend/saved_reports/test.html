Groundwater Status Report – NAGOD

A simple explanation of groundwater availability and usage over three assessment years.

What is this report about?

This report explains how much groundwater is available (recharge) and how much is being used (draft) in the NAGOD area over three different years. Even though the area is still considered safe, the numbers show small but important changes that need attention.

Key Findings (In Simple Words)

  • Groundwater recharge changes every year depending on rainfall and natural conditions.
  • Water usage (draft) is highest in non-command areas like private wells and borewells.
  • Even when groundwater status is marked as “Safe”, usage trends must be monitored.
  • One year shows higher extraction, which could become risky if repeated.

What does this mean for NAGOD?

Right now, NAGOD is still in a safe groundwater category. However, the data shows that groundwater usage is high and can increase if not controlled.

If extraction continues to rise and recharge decreases in future years, the area may move towards a semi-critical or critical stage.

Simple Suggestions

  • Encourage rainwater harvesting in villages and towns.
  • Monitor borewell usage in non-command areas.
  • Promote water-saving farming techniques.
  • Review groundwater data every year, not just category labels.
