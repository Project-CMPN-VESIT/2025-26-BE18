Groundwater Sustainability Analysis - Lucknow

Impact of Land Use Change Dynamics using Earth Observation Data (2008-2016)

Executive Summary

This analysis, based on multi-temporal satellite and field survey data from 2008 to 2016, reveals significant changes in land use/land cover (LULC) and hydro-geomorphic features in the Lucknow area of the Ganga plain. The study highlights a concerning trend of groundwater depletion and loss of natural water bodies. Built-up land and open land/construction areas have increased substantially (4.09% and 5.49% respectively), leading to a decrease in vegetation cover (-8.80%) and agricultural land (-2.31%). Crucially, surface water bodies decreased by 0.35%, and major hydro-geomorphic features like paleochannels and oxbow lakes experienced significant area reductions (24.13 km² to 15.34 km² for paleochannels, 6.78 km² to 5.23 km² for oxbow lakes, and 17.46 km² to 8.98 km² for water bodies). These changes are directly correlated with declining groundwater levels, with areas having a depth to water level >35 meters doubling from 1 km² to 2 km² and areas with 5-10 mbgl increasing from 504 km² to 645 km². The findings underscore the severe pressure on water resources due to urbanization, extensive agricultural practices, and altered natural recharge, emphasizing the urgent need for sustainable management.

Key Action Items

  • **Prioritize Recharge:** Implement strategies to enhance natural groundwater recharge, counteract the effects of increased impervious surfaces.
  • **Protect Water Bodies:** Undertake urgent inventory, rejuvenation, and restoration of natural ponds, lakes, streams, and other hydro-geomorphic features.
  • **Sustainable Urban Planning:** Integrate water resource management with urban planning to control unchecked expansion of built-up areas and open land/construction.
  • **Regulate Groundwater Use:** Develop policies to manage extensive groundwater exploration for agriculture and other domestic purposes.
  • **Promote Green Infrastructure:** Increase vegetation cover and green spaces within urban areas to improve infiltration and reduce runoff.
  • **Monitor & Data-Driven Decisions:** Continue spatio-temporal monitoring of LULC changes, hydro-geomorphic units, and groundwater levels to inform policy and decision-making for water resources management.
