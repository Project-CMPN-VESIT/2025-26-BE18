Groundwater Sustainability Analysis: Lucknow

Executive Summary

This analysis investigates the long-term impact of land use/land cover (LULC) changes on surface and groundwater resources in the Lucknow area (Ganga plain) between 2008 and 2016, utilizing multi-temporal satellite and field survey data. The study reveals significant anthropogenic pressures driving substantial changes, including rapid urbanization, agricultural expansion, and depletion of natural water bodies and hydro-geomorphic features. Key findings indicate a substantial increase in built-up land (4.09%) and open/construction land (5.49%), paralleled by an 8.80% decrease in vegetation cover, a 2.31% decrease in agricultural land, and a 0.35% reduction in surface water bodies. Hydro-geomorphic features crucial for groundwater recharge, such as oxbow lakes and paleochannels, have also seen significant reductions (e.g., paleochannels decreased from 24.13 km² to 15.34 km²). Consequently, groundwater levels have shown an alarming declining trend, with an increasing area reporting deeper water levels (e.g., the area with groundwater depth >30 meters below ground level significantly increased, from 16 km² in 2008 to 45 km² in 2016). This rapid depletion is strongly correlated with LULC changes, highlighting an urgent need for sustainable water resource management strategies.

Key Action Items

  • Prioritize sustainable urban planning to curb unchecked expansion of impervious surfaces, which hinder groundwater recharge.
  • Implement aggressive reforestation and green cover initiatives to enhance natural infiltration and groundwater recharge, countering the 8.80% vegetation loss.
  • Develop and enforce policies for the protection and rejuvenation of critical hydro-geomorphic features (e.g., paleochannels, oxbow lakes, surface water bodies) that have shown significant decline.
  • Promote efficient water use practices in agriculture to reduce reliance on groundwater extraction, especially given the continuous decrease in agricultural land.
  • Establish a robust groundwater monitoring network and integrated database to inform evidence-based decision-making for water resource management.
  • Investigate alternative water sources and rainwater harvesting to supplement groundwater resources and mitigate depletion.
