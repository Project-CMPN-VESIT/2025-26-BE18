Impact of Land Use Change on Groundwater Sustainability (Lucknow Area)

Executive Summary

This analysis, based on multi-temporal satellite and field survey data for the Lucknow area from 2008 to 2016, reveals significant impacts of Land Use/Land Cover (LULC) changes on groundwater resources and hydro-geomorphic features. Rapid urbanization and extensive agricultural practices have led to a substantial alteration of the landscape. The study observed a 4.09% increase in built-up land and a 5.49% increase in open land, primarily at the expense of natural vegetation, which saw an 8.80% decrease, and agricultural land, which decreased by 2.31%. Surface water bodies also experienced a 0.35% reduction. Crucially, vital hydro-geomorphic features like paleochannels and oxbow lakes have significantly declined, impacting natural recharge. Groundwater levels show an alarming decreasing trend, with areas experiencing depths of greater than 20 meters below ground level increasing by 48.3% (from 116 km² to 172 km²) during the study period. A strong positive correlation (R²=0.81) was found between LULC changes and groundwater level dynamics, underscoring the urgent need for sustainable management.

Key Action Items

  • Implement sustainable water resource management strategies to address declining groundwater levels.
  • Prioritize the protection, rejuvenation, and restoration of natural water bodies and hydro-geomorphic features like paleochannels and oxbow lakes.
  • Develop and enforce proper urban planning policies that minimize impervious surfaces and promote natural infiltration.
  • Focus on the rejuvenation of major tributaries of the Gomati River basin to enhance surface and groundwater resources.
  • Continue and expand monitoring of LULC changes and groundwater dynamics using geospatial technology to inform decision-making.
