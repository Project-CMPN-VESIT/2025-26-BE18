Executive Summary: Kalyan Groundwater

The groundwater situation in Kalyan has consistently remained in a "safe" category over the past several years (2016-17 to 2024-25). This is great news! It means that the amount of water people take out from underground (what we call 'draft') is much less than the amount of water that naturally refills the ground (called 'recharge'). While there was a lot of rain in 2016-17, rainfall levels have been more consistent and lower since 2019-20. Even with this, the natural refilling of groundwater has stayed quite high, though it has shown a slight, gradual decrease recently. The amount of water taken out (draft) was higher in 2016-17 but significantly reduced in later years and has stayed relatively stable since then, which is key to keeping Kalyan's groundwater in the "safe" zone.

Key Insights

  • Consistently Safe: Kalyan's groundwater is in a good, sustainable state, meaning we're not using more than what nature provides.
  • Slight Decrease in Refill: The natural refilling of groundwater (recharge) has slowly gone down from about 866 Million Cubic Meters (MCM) in 2019-20 to around 852 MCM in 2024-25. This is a trend to watch.
  • Sensible Water Use: After using a higher amount of water in 2016-17 (about 394 MCM), the community has significantly cut down and maintained stable usage, around 163-165 MCM in recent years. This smart usage is vital for sustainability.
  • Low Usage Percentage: Only about 19-20% of the naturally refilled groundwater is being used. This low percentage is excellent for ensuring water availability for future generations.
