Understanding Groundwater Status

Groundwater refers to the water stored beneath the Earth's surface in soil pores and in the fractures of rock formations. Its level depends on two main factors:

  • Recharge: This is the amount of water that naturally replenishes the underground reservoirs. It primarily comes from rainfall seeping into the ground, but can also include water from rivers, lakes, and irrigation.
  • Draft: This is the total amount of groundwater being pumped out or withdrawn for various uses, such as farming, drinking, and industrial purposes.

The difference between Recharge and Draft tells us how healthy our groundwater system is.

Category "Safe": When the amount of water recharged into the ground is significantly more than what is being drawn out, the area is categorized as "safe." This means there's a good surplus of groundwater, ensuring long-term sustainability. Shirol consistently falls into this desirable category.
