Groundwater Sustainability Analysis: Lucknow

Impact of Land Use Change Dynamics using Earth Observation Data (2008-2016)

Executive Summary

This analysis, based on multi-temporal satellite and field survey data from 2008 to 2016, reveals significant anthropogenic pressure on groundwater resources in Lucknow, Ganga plain. The region has experienced rapid urbanization and extensive agricultural practices, leading to profound changes in Land Use/Land Cover (LULC) and hydro-geomorphic features. Built-up land and open/construction areas increased substantially by 4.09% and 5.49% respectively, at the expense of vegetation cover (-8.80%) and agricultural land (-2.31%). Surface water bodies also saw a decline of 0.35%. Crucially, hydro-geomorphic features vital for water recharge, such as paleochannels (-36.4%) and oxbow lakes (-22.7%), decreased significantly. This LULC transformation, coupled with increased water demand, has resulted in an alarming depletion of groundwater levels across the area, indicating a critical threat to water resource sustainability.

Key Action Items

  • Sustainable Urban Planning: Implement stringent urban planning policies to control rampant built-up expansion, especially in areas critical for groundwater recharge.
  • Protection of Natural Features: Prioritize the protection and rejuvenation of existing surface water bodies and hydro-geomorphic features (e.g., paleochannels, oxbow lakes) to enhance natural infiltration and recharge.
  • Water Resource Management: Develop and implement effective water conservation strategies, including rainwater harvesting and efficient irrigation techniques, to reduce reliance on groundwater.
  • Environmental Monitoring: Continue long-term monitoring of LULC changes, groundwater levels, and hydro-geomorphic features using advanced geospatial technologies to inform adaptive management.
  • Public Awareness: Launch campaigns to raise public awareness about the declining water resources and the importance of sustainable water use and land management.
