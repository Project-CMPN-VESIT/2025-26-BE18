Groundwater Level Analysis: Shirol

Understanding Groundwater Trends for Sustainable Management

Executive Summary

This analysis examines the groundwater situation in Shirol based on data from 2016-17 to 2024-25. The key metrics observed are groundwater recharge (water entering the ground) and draft (water extracted from the ground). The net balance (recharge minus draft) gives us an indication of the overall change in groundwater storage. Shirol has consistently been categorized as "safe" in terms of groundwater availability. While there was a significant drop in net groundwater balance between 2016-17 and 2019-20, the subsequent years show a relatively stable to slightly increasing trend in net balance.

Key Insights

  • Safe Category: Shirol has been consistently categorized as 'safe', indicating that groundwater extraction is within sustainable limits relative to recharge.
  • Initial Decline: A notable decrease in net groundwater balance occurred between 2016-17 and 2019-20, largely due to a significant reduction in reported rainfall and a corresponding, though smaller, reduction in draft.
  • Recent Stability/Slight Increase: From 2019-20 onwards, the net groundwater balance has shown a trend of minor fluctuations but a slight overall increase, suggesting a period of relative stability or cautious recovery after the initial drop.
  • Recharge & Draft Balance: Annual recharge values have remained relatively stable from 2019-20, hovering around 11,400 to 11,500 units. Similarly, draft levels have also stabilized around 4,100 to 4,200 units.
  • Prediction for 2025-26: Based on the observed trends, the net groundwater balance for Shirol is predicted to be approximately 7297 units, maintaining its 'safe' status.
