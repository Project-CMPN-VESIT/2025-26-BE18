Groundwater Status: Shirol

Executive Summary

The groundwater situation in Shirol is currently in a safe category. This means that the region has sufficient groundwater resources, and the amount of water being used is less than what's naturally replenished. This indicates a healthy and sustainable groundwater environment.

Please Note: Detailed groundwater level data (like the depth to water table) was not available in the provided information. This analysis focuses on the overall water balance, including how much water is added back (recharge) and how much is taken out (draft).

Key Insights

  • Healthy Usage: Only about 38.6% of the total available groundwater is being extracted. This is a very good sign, as it shows the region is not over-pumping its underground water reserves.
  • Strong Natural Replenishment: A large volume of water flows back into the ground (recharge). The primary sources are return flows from surface irrigation (around 66%) and direct rainfall infiltration (around 21%).
  • Agriculture is the Main Consumer: The majority of the groundwater taken out (approximately 95%) is used for farming. Domestic use accounts for a smaller portion, and industrial use is negligible.
  • Good for the Future: There's a substantial amount of groundwater (over 6,600 Million Cubic Meters) available for future use, which helps ensure water security for Shirol.
