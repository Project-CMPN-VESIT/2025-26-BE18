Groundwater Sustainability Analysis (2008-2016)

An analysis of long-term Land Use/Land Cover (LULC) changes and their impact on groundwater resources and hydro-geomorphic features in the Lucknow area of the Ganga plain, utilizing multi-temporal satellite and field survey data.

Executive Summary

The study reveals significant transformations in the Lucknow area between 2008 and 2016, primarily driven by rapid urbanization, population growth, and extensive agricultural practices. Built-up land and open land/construction areas experienced substantial increases of 4.09% and 5.49% respectively, indicating a surge in development. Concurrently, vital natural resources suffered, with vegetation cover decreasing by 8.80%, agricultural land by 2.31%, and surface water bodies by 0.35%.

Hydro-geomorphic features, crucial for natural water storage and recharge, also showed a concerning decline. Features like paleochannels, oxbow lakes, and cut-off meanders saw considerable reductions in their area. This loss of permeable surfaces and natural water bodies has severely impacted groundwater recharge, leading to a noticeable depletion of groundwater levels across the region. Areas with deeper groundwater (>35 mbgl) expanded significantly, while shallower water zones (<5 mbgl) shrank. The strong positive correlation (R²=0.81) between LULC changes (especially urbanization) and groundwater level dynamics underscores the urgent need for sustainable water resource management and urban planning to mitigate these environmental impacts.

Key Action Items

  • Prioritize sustainable urban planning to control haphazard expansion and protect natural recharge areas.
  • Implement measures for the rejuvenation and restoration of surface water bodies, including natural ponds, lakes, and streams.
  • Develop strategies to enhance natural infiltration and groundwater recharge, potentially through green infrastructure.
  • Monitor LULC changes and groundwater levels regularly using geospatial technologies to inform decision-making.
  • Promote water conservation and efficient water use practices in both agricultural and urban sectors to reduce reliance on groundwater.
  • Protect and restore hydro-geomorphic features like paleochannels and oxbow lakes, recognizing their role in water resource management.
