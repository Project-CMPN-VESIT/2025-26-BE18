Geology (Rock Type)

The primary rock type in your area is Basalt (Deccan Trap). Basalt is a hard, volcanic rock. While dense, it typically holds groundwater in specific features:

  • Fractured Zones: Cracks and fissures in the rock act as pathways and storage for water.
  • Weathered Layers: The upper layers of basalt can weather into softer material that is permeable.
  • Interflow Zones: Layers between different basalt flows can also serve as aquifers.

This geology offers a moderate groundwater potential, meaning that with careful siting to target these fractured and weathered zones, a borewell can be successful.

Final Recommendation & Precautionary Measures

Our final recommendation is a "CONDITIONAL YES" for a borewell.

To maximize your chances of success and ensure sustainable water access, we strongly recommend the following critical steps:

  • Mandatory Geophysical Survey: Before any drilling commences, engage a qualified geophysicist to conduct a detailed Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT) or similar survey at your specific plot. This will help pinpoint the exact location and depth of fractured zones most likely to yield water. This step is crucial and significantly improves the chances of a successful borewell.
  • Rainwater Harvesting: Implement robust rainwater harvesting systems. Given the typically high annual rainfall in the Thane region (even if recent data points were lower), capturing and storing this water will reduce reliance on groundwater and enhance overall water security.
  • Artificial Recharge Pits: Construct recharge pits or trenches. These structures will facilitate the infiltration of collected rainwater and surface runoff directly into the fractured basalt aquifer, actively replenishing groundwater levels.
  • Controlled Extraction & Monitoring: If a borewell is installed, implement a pumping regime that monitors water levels. Avoid over-extraction to prevent rapid depletion of the aquifer and ensure long-term sustainability. Seasonal usage planning is advisable.
  • Consult Local Authorities: Before proceeding, always consult with local water resource departments or Panchayat/Municipal authorities to understand any specific regulations, permits, or guidelines regarding borewell drilling and groundwater extraction in your area.

By following these precautions, you can proceed with greater confidence and contribute to the sustainable management of groundwater resources in your area.
