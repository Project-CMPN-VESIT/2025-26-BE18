Groundwater Resources Sustainability Analysis

Impact of Land Use Change Dynamics in Lucknow Area

Executive Summary

This analysis, based on multi-temporal satellite data (2008 & 2016) and field surveys, reveals significant environmental changes in the Lucknow area of the Ganga plain. The region has experienced rapid urbanization and extensive agricultural practices, leading to substantial alterations in land use/land cover (LULC) and hydro-geomorphic features. Key findings indicate a concerning decline in natural water resources:

  • Built-up land increased by 4.09% (from 176.38 to 279.89 km²) and open land/construction increased by 5.49% (from 195.45 to 334.37 km²), reflecting rapid urban expansion.
  • Conversely, vegetation cover decreased by 8.80% (from 434.33 to 211.71 km²) and agricultural land by 2.31% (from 1407.8 to 1349.49 km²).
  • Crucially, surface water bodies declined by 0.35% (overall LULC class, from 24.53 to 15.77 km²) and hydro-geomorphic water bodies by a more significant margin (from 17.46 km² to 8.98 km²). Paleochannels (24.13 to 15.34 km²) and oxbow lakes (6.78 to 5.23 km²) also saw considerable reductions.
  • These LULC changes, combined with intensive groundwater exploration, have resulted in a widespread depletion of groundwater levels. Areas with very shallow water tables (<5 mbgl) halved (from 58 to 29 km²), while deep water levels (>35 mbgl) doubled (from 1 to 2 km²), indicating a significant shift towards deeper groundwater.
  • The study establishes a positive correlation (R²=0.81) between LULC changes and groundwater level dynamics, underscoring the direct impact of human activities on water resources.

Overall, the findings highlight an alarming trend of environmental degradation and a serious threat to the sustainability of water resources in the region, necessitating urgent management interventions.

Key Action Items

  • Implement sustainable urban planning to regulate unchecked expansion and protect natural infiltration areas.
  • Prioritize the rejuvenation and restoration of depleted surface water bodies, including natural ponds, lakes, and rivers, to enhance natural recharge.
  • Develop and enforce policies for efficient groundwater management to reduce over-extraction, especially for agricultural and domestic purposes.
  • Protect and restore hydro-geomorphic features like paleochannels and oxbow lakes, which are crucial for water storage and groundwater recharge.
  • Promote green infrastructure development and increase vegetation cover to improve surface water infiltration and mitigate urban heat island effects.
  • Establish a robust water resource database to support informed decision-making and continuous monitoring of LULC and groundwater dynamics.
